A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

Date of Birth:15 October 1931, Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu

Date of Death:27 July 2015(age 83 years), Shillong, Meghalaya

Nationality: Indian

Occupation: Aerospace Scientist, Author, 11th President of India(2002-2007)

Early Life

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born into a humble family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. Despite financial difficulties, his parents encouraged his education, and Kalam had a strong fascination for learning from an early age. He worked hard in school and graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later in aerospace engineering from Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).

Career as a Scientist:

Dr. Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he became a key figure in India's space and defense programs. He played a crucial role in developing ballistic missile technology and was thus called the "Missile Man of India." He led the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles, solidifying India's position as a growing power in missile technology. His contributions were pivotal to India's 1998 Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which made India a nuclear-armed state.

Presidency:

In 2002, Dr. Kalam became the 11 th President of India, where he was affectionately known as the "People's President." He was immensely popular among all sections of society due to his simplicity, humility, and vision for India's development, particularly in science and technology.

Awards:

  • Padma Bhushan(1981)
  • Padma Vibhushan(1990)
  • Bharat Ratna(1997)
  • Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration(1997)
  • Veer Savarkar Award(1998)
  • Ramanujan Award(2000)
  • King Charles II Medal(2007)
  • Hoover Medal(2009)
  • International von Karman Wings Award(2009)
  • IEEE Honorary Membership(2011)
  • NSS Wernher Von Braun Memorial Award(2013)

Books and Inspiration:

Dr. Kalam was not just a scientist and statesman, but also an author. His books, including Wings of Fire (his autobiography), Ignited Minds, and India 2020, have inspired millions, particularly the youth of India. His vision for India was deeply rooted in empowering the younger generation to dream big and work hard toward national development.

Death and Legacy:

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong. His sudden death was mourned nationwide. He is remembered as a visionary leader, a humble human being, and a scientist who gave his life to his country. His birthday, October 15, is celebrated as World Students' Day in his honor. His work continues to inspire the next generation to pursue science, education, and service to the nation.
